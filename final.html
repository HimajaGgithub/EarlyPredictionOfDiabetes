Type 1

Type 1 diabetes is a chronic autoimmune condition in which the immune system mistakenly attacks and destroys the insulin-producing cells in the pancreas called beta cells. As a result, the pancreas produces little to no insulin, leading to high blood sugar levels.

Type 2

Type 2 diabetes is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by high blood sugar levels resulting from insulin resistance and insufficient insulin production. Unlike Type 1 diabetes, which is an autoimmune condition, Type 2 diabetes is often influenced by lifestyle factors and genetic predisposition.

Type 3

Type 3 diabetes is a term that has been proposed to describe a potential link between Alzheimer's disease and insulin resistance. However, it is not an officially recognized medical term or diagnosis. The concept of Type 3 diabetes is still a subject of ongoing research and debate in the scientific community.
